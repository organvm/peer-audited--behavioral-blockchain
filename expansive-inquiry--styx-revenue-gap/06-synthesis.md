## Productive Contradictions

### 1. The Product IS the Governance vs. The Governance IS the Block
**Logic** says: the governance system is the actual product — sell it as B2B SaaS.
**Mythos** says: the governance system is the block — ritual displacement prevents product formation.

These are not contradictory. They are the same observation at different altitudes. Logic sees the governance system as a *salable artifact*. Mythos sees it as a *behavioral pattern* that prevents market contact. Both are true: the governance framework *could* be sold (Logic), but the *act of governing* prevents the builder from selling it (Mythos). The contradiction resolves when you separate the artifact from the behavior: the audit framework is the product, but the governance habit is the disease.

### 2. "Almost Done" vs. "Step Zero"
**Scope** says: a product that has never been deployed is at step zero, not "almost done."
**Bridge** says: the seed is genetically complete — it *is* a plant in suspended animation.

Both are true at different levels of description. At the market level, Styx is at step zero (no users, no revenue, no distribution). At the engineering level, Styx is complete (tests pass, governance GRADUATED). The contradiction reveals that "done" is not a property of the artifact but a property of the *relationship between artifact and environment*. A seed in a vault is done as a seed and not-started as a plant. Styx is done as a system and not-started as a product.

### 3. "Find the Squid" vs. "Extract as B2B"
**Bridge** says: embed Styx's audit engine inside an existing community with peer density.
**Logic** says: extract the audit framework and sell it as B2B SaaS to projects that need peer verification.

These are two different go-to-market strategies for the same insight (the audit framework is the real product). Bridge says *embed* (parasitic, low-friction, requires finding the right host). Logic says *sell* (transactional, higher-friction, requires a sales motion). The contradiction is strategic, not logical: should Styx be a plugin or a product? The answer depends on whether the builder has distribution relationships (sell) or technical integration capacity (embed).

### 2. Parasitic Readiness
The system requires an external host (peers, community, market density) that it cannot generate internally. The "almost done" state is not a transitional phase. It is a *stable equilibrium* that requires no risk. **Implication:** the system will not deploy itself. It requires an external force — a circuit breaker, a deadline, a customer conversation — that makes containment more costly than exposure.

## The Cut

The Möbius strip doesn't need a direction. It needs a cut.

The cut is a change in what counts as validation:

**Before the cut:** validation = tests pass, docs are audited, governance is GRADUATED.
**After the cut:** validation = someone outside this directory gives us money.

Not "someone might give us money if we deploy." Not "we are ready for someone to give us money." Actual money from an actual person who is not the builder.

Until that cut is made, every additional action — including this inquiry — is the Möbius strip continuing its surface.
