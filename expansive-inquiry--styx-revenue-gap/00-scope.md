## Core Inquiry

**Given a feature-complete but undeployed product drowning in governance overhead with zero market traction, what is the minimum viable path from "GRADUATED repository" to "revenue-generating system," and is the peer-audited behavioral market model structurally compatible with the distribution channels required to reach users?**

## Hidden Ambiguities

- **"Peer-audited" assumes peers exist.** With 0 stars, 0 forks, and no external community, the peer audit mechanism is either (a) a simulation, (b) performed by the builder themselves, or (c) not yet activated. The product may be structurally incomplete until real peers arrive — which it cannot attract without deployment — which it cannot risk without peers auditing. This is a bootstrap paradox, not a feature gap.

- **"Behavioral market" is underspecified.** Does this mean users trade behavioral commitments? Wager on outcomes? The linguistic cloaker (stake→commitment, bet→vault) suggests the underlying mechanic resembles gambling, which creates a fundamental tension: if the real mechanic is gambling-adjacent, App Store compliance isn't a tension — it's a hard block. The cloaker may be solving a problem that has no solution on iOS.

- **"v0.9.0-beta-ready" is a self-assessment.** 1,107 passing tests prove internal consistency, not market readiness. Who defined the 0.9.0 criteria? Were they defined by user needs or by the builder's internal specification? Beta-ready for whom?

- **The question "why is it not making money?" presupposes it should be.** A product that has never been deployed, never been used by a non-builder, and has no distribution channel is not "almost done" — it is at step zero. The framing conflates engineering completeness with product existence.

- **The three sub-questions may be one question in disguise.** The scaling question, the App Store question, and the "why no money" question all collapse into: *Is there a viable path to users for this product on any platform, and if not, what would need to change about the product itself (not the governance, not the tests) to make one?*
