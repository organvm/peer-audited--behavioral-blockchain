# Audience-as-Product Model — Jessica's Following

Instantiation of `playbook--audience-growth-engine.md` §5 for Jessica. Treats her following as
its own product line — valuable and monetizable even if it never converts a single Styx user —
while staying a clean funnel into the Styx beta.

## Why two products from one audience

The $100/hr Jessica is paying builds **one audience** that serves **two products**:
1. **Styx** — the beta gets a warm, high-intent, pre-trusted top-of-funnel.
2. **The following itself** — an owned media asset Jessica controls (and co-owns the system for).

If Styx pivots, slows, or stays niche, the audience asset still has standalone value. That
de-risks the whole engagement.

## The monetization ladder (Playbook §5)

```
free follow → email subscriber → lead magnet → low-ticket offer → community/cohort → sponsorship/affiliate
```

| Rung | Jessica instance | On now? | Notes |
|------|------------------|---------|-------|
| Free follow | TikTok/IG/etc. short-form (the Host channel) | ✅ | top of funnel; grow reach |
| Email subscriber | the no-contact guide opt-in | ✅ | builds P4 owned list — the real asset |
| Lead magnet | "No-Contact 30-Day Survival Guide" + "Do Not Text Your Ex Tonight" tool | ✅ | free, high-value; converts follow → email |
| Low-ticket offer | a small paid recovery resource (e.g. a guided 30-day workbook) | ⏳ Day 60+ | proves willingness to pay; funds the engine |
| Community / cohort | the no-contact cohort (shared with Styx's first room) | ⏳ Day 30+ | recurring value; doubles as Styx Cohort 1 |
| Sponsorship / affiliate | aligned brand/creator partnerships | ⏳ later | only once reach is large + trusted |

## Where Styx sits

Styx is the **flagship CTA beside the low-ticket rung**, not a replacement for the free value.
The order of operations protects trust:
1. Give away genuine help (lead magnets, free content) — earn the relationship.
2. Invite into Styx as the *optional accountability layer* for people who want structure.
3. Only then introduce paid audience products, positioned as "more from Jessica," never as a
   bait-and-switch off the free promise.

## Coexistence rules (protect the asset and the beta)

- **Free value first, always.** The guide and the hard-night tool are free, full stop. No
  gating the thing that earns trust.
- **No cannibalization.** A paid Jessica product must not compete with or confuse the Styx beta
  ask. If both appear in one week, separate them by audience intent (struggler-in-crisis →
  free tool + beta; engaged-and-stable → low-ticket).
- **No exploitation.** This is an emotionally vulnerable audience. No fear-based upsells, no
  manufactured urgency around someone's heartbreak, no implying a purchase fixes the
  relationship. (Inherits REGE CRIT:content-quality + the niche sensitivity rule.)
- **Consent for proof.** Any testimonial / success story needs written consent (REGE #3).

## What this protects against

- **Platform risk:** the email list (P4) means a deplatform or algorithm change doesn't erase
  the asset.
- **Product risk:** if Styx stays small, the following still earns.
- **Trust risk:** sequencing free-value-first keeps the audience warm for *both* products.
