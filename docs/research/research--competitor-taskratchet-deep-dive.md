## 3. Gap Identification (The "Shatter Point")

### Shatter Point 1: The "Honesty Gap"
*   **The Bug:** As the stakes increase, the temptation to lie increases proportionally. If a user stakes $100 on "Clean the Garage" and fails, clicking "Done" saves them $100 with zero consequences. TaskRatchet assumes the user is an "Honest Actor," which is a dangerous assumption in high-stakes behavioral markets.
*   **The Opportunity:** Styx's **Fury Network** converts the user into a "Suspect" and the auditor into a "Bounty Hunter." We require **Digital Exhaust** or **Artifact Proof** (e.g., a photo of the clean garage with a date stamp) that is audited by a peer.

### Shatter Point 2: Lack of Contextual Guardrails
*   **The Bug:** TaskRatchet doesn't care *what* the task is. You could stake money on something unhealthy or illegal. It lacks the psychological and legal safety protocols of a specialized behavioral platform.
*   **The Opportunity:** Styx's **Aegis Protocol** and **Recovery Protocol** provide niche-specific guardrails (e.g., 3-target cap for No Contact, mandatory accountability partners) that ensure the "Sting" is used for growth, not self-harm.

### Shatter Point 3: Zero Gamification
*   **The Bug:** TaskRatchet is a tool, not an experience. It lacks the long-term "Stickiness" of a gamified journey. Once the immediate task is done, the user has little incentive to return until the next crisis.
*   **The Opportunity:** Styx uses **Integrity Scores** and **RPG Progression** to turn the "Blockchain of Truth" into a status game. Users aren't just checking off boxes; they are building a verifiable reputation for integrity.
