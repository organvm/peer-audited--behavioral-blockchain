The unifying evolutionary claim.

When designing a feature, imagine the user as a **controller trying to minimize internal error** under uncertainty.

They will probe your system for leverage.  
They will test boundaries.  
They will respond to incentives faster than instructions.  
They will habituate quickly to rewards.  
They will ignore signals that are noisy or fake.

This is not cynicism. It is accurate modeling.

Your job is not to make people better.  
Your job is to **align their existing drives with real outcomes**.

## VII. A diagnostic you can apply to any feature you design

Before shipping anything, answer these questions in plain language.

Which drive does this feature amplify?  
Which drive constrains it?  
What real-world cost does misuse incur?  
How fast does that cost appear?  
Can users simulate success without actually succeeding?  
Does the system remember abuse?

If you cannot answer those, the feature will eventually fail at scale.
