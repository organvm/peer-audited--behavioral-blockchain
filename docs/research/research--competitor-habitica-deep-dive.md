## 3. Gap Identification (The "Shatter Point")

### Shatter Point 1: Zero-Friction Cheating
*   **The Bug:** Because the game rewards are more salient than the real-world habits, users often click "Done" just to finish a quest or save a friend from "dying" in-game. This creates a "Motivation Debt" where the user has high in-game stats but zero real-world progress.
*   **The Opportunity:** Styx uses **Digital Exhaust** and the **Fury Network**. You cannot "click through" a Styx barrier; you must provide artifact-based proof that is audited by a financially incentivized peer.

### Shatter Point 2: Lack of Real Loss Aversion
*   **The Bug:** Losing a virtual "Longsword" or character HP is not a significant enough sting for high-volatility behavioral change (e.g., ending an addiction or a toxic relationship).
*   **The Opportunity:** Styx adds **Financial Loss Aversion** (monetary stakes) to the gamified layer. We use the RPG mechanics to *visualize* the state of the blockchain, not to substitute for real-world consequences.

### Shatter Point 3: The "Soft Social" Trap
*   **The Bug:** Habitica parties are "too nice." Teammates often forgive each other for cheating because they want to stay friends.
*   **The Opportunity:** Styx's **Fury Network** is anonymous and adversarial. The auditor makes money when they catch the committer cheating. This "Inquisitor" dynamic ensures high-integrity enforcement that a "Party" of friends cannot provide.
